# Disciplinary Action Policy

**Company**: Amazatic Technologies
**Effective Date**: January 2025
**Version**: 1.5

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes fair and consistent procedures for addressing employee misconduct, performance issues, and policy violations while ensuring due process and legal compliance.

## 2. Core Principles

### 2.1 Progressive Discipline
- Start with least severe action
- Escalate if behavior continues
- Opportunity for improvement

### 2.2 Fairness and Consistency
- Same rules apply to everyone
- Similar offenses = similar consequences
- No discrimination or bias

### 2.3 Documentation
- Written record of all disciplinary actions
- Evidence preservation
- Clear communication

### 2.4 Confidentiality
- Disciplinary matters kept confidential
- Disclosed only on need-to-know basis

## 3. Types of Misconduct

### 3.1 Minor Misconduct
- Occasional lateness or absenteeism
- Minor policy violations
- Isolated incidents of poor judgment
- Inappropriate language (non-offensive)
- Dress code violations

**Typical Action**: Verbal warning → Written warning

### 3.2 Serious Misconduct
- Repeated minor misconduct after warnings
- Insubordination
- Unauthorized absence (1-2 days)
- Misuse of company resources
- Violation of safety procedures
- Poor performance despite feedback
- Breach of confidentiality (minor)

**Typical Action**: Written warning → Final written warning → Suspension

### 3.3 Gross Misconduct (Immediate Termination)
- Theft, fraud, or dishonesty
- Physical violence or threats
- Sexual harassment
- Discrimination or hate speech
- Substance abuse at work
- Intentional damage to company property
- Sharing confidential information with competitors
- Forgery of documents
- Bribery or corruption
- Viewing inappropriate content at work
- Unauthorized absence (3+ days)
- Serious safety violations

**Typical Action**: Immediate termination (may bypass progressive discipline)

## 4. Disciplinary Actions

### 4.1 Verbal Warning
- **Purpose**: Address minor, first-time issues
- **Process**:
  1. Private discussion between manager and employee
  2. Explain the issue and expectation
  3. Employee acknowledges understanding
  4. Document the conversation (not given to employee)
- **Impact**: No formal record in HR file
- **Validity**: 3 months

### 4.2 Written Warning
- **Purpose**: Formal notice for repeated or serious issues
- **Process**:
  1. Manager documents the issue in writing
  2. Includes:
     - Description of violation
     - Date, time, location
     - Expected behavior
     - Consequences if repeated
     - Employee right to respond
  3. Meeting with employee to discuss
  4. Employee signs (signature = acknowledgment, not agreement)
  5. Copy to HR file
- **Impact**: Formal record
- **Validity**: 6 months

### 4.3 Final Written Warning
- **Purpose**: Last chance before serious action
- **Process**:
  1. Detailed written document
  2. States clearly this is final warning
  3. Specific improvement plan with timeline
  4. Consequences of non-compliance (suspension or termination)
  5. Meeting with employee, manager, and HR
  6. Employee signature
  7. Original to HR file
- **Impact**: Serious mark on record
- **Validity**: 12 months
- **Note**: During this period, no promotions or salary increments

### 4.4 Suspension Without Pay
- **Purpose**: Time to investigate serious misconduct or cool-off period
- **Duration**: Typically 1-5 days
- **Process**:
  1. Immediate suspension letter
  2. Investigation conducted during suspension
  3. Decision communicated before end of suspension
  4. Possible outcomes: Return to work, further disciplinary action, termination
- **Impact**: Pay deduction + formal record

### 4.5 Demotion
- **Purpose**: Alternative to termination for poor performance
- **Process**:
  1. Offered when performance doesn't meet role requirements
  2. Move to lower-level role with reduced pay
  3. Employee consent required (or resignation)
  4. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) in new role
- **Impact**: Salary reduction, title change

### 4.6 Termination
- **Purpose**: End employment for gross misconduct or repeated violations
- **Types**:
  - **Termination for cause**: Immediate (gross misconduct)
  - **Termination for poor performance**: After PIP failure
  - **Termination for repeated policy violations**: After progressive discipline
- **Process**:
  1. Thorough investigation and documentation
  2. Legal review (HR + Legal team)
  3. Termination meeting (manager + HR)
  4. Termination letter with reasons
  5. Exit formalities
- **Impact**: Employment ends, no severance (for cause), legal implications

## 5. Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)

### 5.1 When PIP is Used
- Consistent underperformance
- Skills gap
- Behavioral issues affecting work

### 5.2 PIP Components
1. **Clear performance gaps**: Specific issues documented
2. **Measurable goals**: SMART goals for improvement
3. **Timeline**: Typically 30-90 days
4. **Support**: Resources, training, coaching
5. **Check-ins**: Weekly meetings with manager
6. **Evaluation**: Final assessment at end of PIP

### 5.3 PIP Outcomes
- **Successful**: Continue in role, PIP closed
- **Partially successful**: Extend PIP by 30 days
- **Unsuccessful**: Demotion or termination

## 6. Investigation Process

### 6.1 When Investigation is Required
- Harassment or discrimination complaints
- Theft or fraud allegations
- Policy violations with conflicting accounts
- Any serious misconduct

### 6.2 Investigation Steps
1. **Complaint received**: Document in writing
2. **Immediate action**: Separate parties if needed
3. **Appoint investigator**: HR or external (for senior roles)
4. **Gather evidence**: Documents, emails, CCTV, logs
5. **Interview witnesses**: Complainant, accused, witnesses
6. **Maintain confidentiality**: Only inform those who need to know
7. **Document findings**: Investigation report
8. **Decision**: Within 10 business days
9. **Action**: Disciplinary action if warranted
10. **Communication**: To involved parties

### 6.3 Timeline
- Simple cases: 5-7 business days
- Complex cases: Up to 30 days
- Extensions communicated if needed

### 6.4 Suspended During Investigation
- For serious allegations (harassment, theft, violence)
- With pay (unless charged criminally)
- Duration: Until investigation concludes

## 7. Employee Rights During Disciplinary Process

### 7.1 Right to Explanation
- Understand what policy was violated
- Receive specific details of allegations

### 7.2 Right to Respond
- Provide their side of the story
- Submit written response
- Present evidence

### 7.3 Right to Representation
- Have colleague present (not lawyer) in meetings
- Union representative (if applicable)

### 7.4 Right to Appeal
- All disciplinary actions can be appealed
- Appeal within 5 business days
- Reviewed by higher authority

### 7.5 Right to Confidentiality
- Disciplinary matters not shared publicly
- No retaliation for complaints

## 8. Appeal Process

### 8.1 Who Can Appeal
- Employee who received disciplinary action
- Must appeal within 5 business days

### 8.2 Appeal Submission
- Written appeal to HR
- State reasons for appeal
- Provide supporting evidence

### 8.3 Appeal Review
- Reviewed by senior manager or HR director (not involved in original decision)
- Review within 10 business days
- May interview employee and witnesses
- Decision is final

### 8.4 Appeal Outcomes
- Uphold original decision
- Reduce severity of action
- Overturn decision (no action)

## 9. Special Situations

### 9.1 Attendance Issues
- **First offense**: Verbal warning
- **Second offense** (within 3 months): Written warning
- **Third offense**: Final written warning
- **Fourth offense**: Termination

### 9.2 Performance Issues
- Regular feedback and coaching first
- If no improvement: PIP
- PIP failure: Demotion or termination

### 9.3 Code of Conduct Violations
- Depends on severity
- Range from warning to immediate termination

### 9.4 Manager Misconduct
- Same process applies
- Investigated by HR + senior leadership
- Higher standard of conduct expected

## 10. Retaliation Prohibition

- **Prohibited**: Retaliation against employees who report misconduct or participate in investigations
- **Examples of retaliation**: Demotion, poor review, exclusion, hostility
- **Consequences**: Termination for retaliation

## 11. Record Keeping

### 11.1 What is Documented
- All disciplinary actions
- Investigation reports
- Employee responses
- Appeal outcomes

### 11.2 Where Records are Kept
- Confidential HR file
- Restricted access (HR only)

### 11.3 How Long Records are Kept
- Verbal warnings: Not in official file (manager note only)
- Written warnings: 6 months (then purged if no further issues)
- Final written warnings: 12 months
- Terminations: Permanent

## 12. Manager Responsibilities

### 12.1 Address Issues Promptly
- Don't ignore misconduct
- Act within 48 hours of becoming aware

### 12.2 Document Everything
- Keep contemporaneous notes
- Document date, time, witnesses, what was said/done

### 12.3 Consistency
- Apply rules fairly to all team members
- No favoritism

### 12.4 Consult HR
- Before issuing written warnings or above
- For complex situations
- For legal implications

### 12.5 Follow Process
- No "off the books" disciplinary actions
- Follow company procedures

## 13. HR Responsibilities

### 13.1 Policy Guidance
- Advise managers on appropriate actions
- Ensure legal compliance
- Maintain consistency

### 13.2 Investigation
- Conduct or oversee investigations
- Ensure fair process

### 13.3 Documentation
- Maintain disciplinary records
- Prepare documentation for legal defense if needed

### 13.4 Training
- Train managers on disciplinary procedures
- Update policy as laws change

## 14. Examples of Violations and Typical Actions

### 14.1 Attendance
- 1 unauthorized absence: Verbal warning
- 2-3 absences: Written warning
- 4+ absences: Termination

### 14.2 Performance
- One poor quarter: Coaching
- Two poor quarters: PIP
- PIP failure: Demotion or termination

### 14.3 Policy Violations
- Minor (dress code): Verbal warning
- Serious (data breach): Final warning or termination
- Gross misconduct (theft): Immediate termination

### 14.4 Behavioral Issues
- Unprofessional language: Verbal warning
- Insubordination: Written warning
- Harassment: Suspension + termination

## 15. Immediate Termination Guidelines

### 15.1 When Immediate Termination is Appropriate
- Gross misconduct (see Section 3.3)
- After thorough investigation and evidence
- Legal review completed
- CEO/CHRO approval

### 15.2 Process
1. Immediate suspension (with pay) during investigation
2. Investigation (typically 3-7 days)
3. Decision meeting (manager + HR + Legal)
4. Termination meeting (if warranted)
5. Exit formalities same day

### 15.3 Termination Meeting
- Keep it brief (15 minutes)
- State decision clearly
- Provide termination letter
- Collect company property
- Escort out of office
- Disable access immediately

## 16. Reinstatement After Termination

### 16.1 When Allowed
- Termination overturned on appeal
- Court order
- Investigation reveals termination was unjustified

### 16.2 Process
- Back pay for time not worked
- Expungement of termination from record
- Public communication (if termination was public)

## 17. Legal Compliance

### 17.1 Applicable Laws
- Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act
- Shops and Establishments Act (State-specific)
- Labor laws (State and Central)

### 17.2 Consultation
- Legal team reviews all terminations
- Employment lawyer consulted for complex cases

## 18. Union and Labor Relations
- Disciplinary process may differ for unionized employees
- Follow collective bargaining agreement
- Union representative present in meetings

## 19. Confidentiality and Privacy

- Disciplinary matters not discussed publicly
- Information shared only with:
  - Employee's direct chain of command
  - HR
  - Legal team (if required)
  - Government authorities (if required by law)
