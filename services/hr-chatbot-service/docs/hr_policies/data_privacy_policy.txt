# Data Privacy and Confidentiality Policy

**Company**: Amazatic Technologies
**Effective Date**: January 2025
**Version**: 3.0

## 1. Purpose

This policy establishes guidelines for protecting confidential information, personal data, intellectual property, and ensuring compliance with data protection laws including GDPR and Indian IT Act.

## 2. Scope

Applies to:
- All employees (permanent, contract, intern)
- Contractors and consultants
- Third-party vendors with data access
- All company data (customer, employee, business, technical)

## 3. Types of Confidential Information

### 3.1 Business Information
- Business strategies and plans
- Financial data (revenue, costs, projections)
- Sales data, pricing, discounts
- Customer lists and contracts
- Marketing plans and campaigns
- Supplier and vendor information
- M&A plans and negotiations

### 3.2 Technical Information
- Source code and algorithms
- Technical designs and architectures
- Patents, inventions, trade secrets
- Product roadmaps
- Research and development data
- Security protocols

### 3.3 Personal Data
- **Employee data**: Name, address, salary, performance, medical records
- **Customer data**: PII (Personally Identifiable Information), payment details, usage data
- **Visitor data**: Access logs, contact information

### 3.4 Intellectual Property
- Software developed by company
- Designs, logos, branding
- Documentation and manuals
- Presentations and training materials

## 4. Data Classification

### 4.1 Public
- Marketing materials
- Public website content
- Press releases
- Job postings

**Handling**: Can be shared publicly

### 4.2 Internal
- General company information
- Internal announcements
- Employee directories
- Policies and procedures

**Handling**: Can be shared within company, not outside

### 4.3 Confidential
- Customer data
- Financial reports (non-public)
- Employee personal data
- Business plans

**Handling**: Need-to-know basis, encrypted storage and transmission

### 4.4 Highly Confidential / Restricted
- Trade secrets
- Merger and acquisition plans
- Security credentials
- Legal documents
- Executive communications

**Handling**: Extremely limited access, MFA required, audit trail

## 5. Employee Data Privacy

### 5.1 Data Collected
- **Personal**: Name, DOB, address, contact, photo, ID proofs
- **Professional**: Resume, qualifications, experience, references
- **Financial**: Bank account, PAN, salary, tax declarations
- **Attendance**: Work hours, leave records, location data
- **Performance**: Reviews, ratings, feedback
- **Health**: Medical records (for insurance), disability information

### 5.2 Purpose of Collection
- Employment administration
- Payroll processing
- Benefits administration
- Performance management
- Legal compliance

### 5.3 Employee Rights
- **Access**: View your personal data (request via HR)
- **Correction**: Update incorrect data
- **Deletion**: Request deletion (post-employment, subject to legal retention requirements)
- **Portability**: Receive data in portable format
- **Objection**: Object to certain processing (e.g., marketing communications)

### 5.4 Data Retention
- **Active employment**: Throughout employment + 7 years post-exit
- **Resumes (not hired)**: 1 year
- **Payroll records**: 7 years
- **Performance records**: 5 years post-exit

## 6. Customer Data Privacy

### 6.1 Data Minimization
- Collect only necessary data
- Purpose-specific collection
- Explicit consent obtained

### 6.2 Data Usage
- Only for agreed purposes
- No selling of customer data
- No sharing without consent (except legal requirements)

### 6.3 Data Security
- Encryption at rest and in transit
- Access controls (role-based)
- Regular security audits
- Vulnerability assessments

### 6.4 Breach Notification
- Customers notified within 72 hours of discovery
- Details of breach, data affected, remediation steps
- Regulatory notification as required

## 7. Confidentiality Obligations

### 7.1 During Employment
- Do not share confidential information outside company
- Share internally only on need-to-know basis
- Use secure channels (company email, encrypted storage)
- Do not discuss in public places
- Lock screens when away from desk

### 7.2 Post-Employment
- Confidentiality obligation continues indefinitely for trade secrets
- 5 years for other confidential information
- Return all company data and documents
- Do not use in new employment

### 7.3 Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- Signed at joining
- Legally binding
- Breach may result in legal action and damages
- Extends to freelancers, contractors, vendors

## 8. Data Handling Best Practices

### 8.1 Email
- Do not send confidential data to personal emails
- Use encryption for sensitive attachments
- BCC for mass emails (protect recipient privacy)
- Auto-delete emails policy: 90 days for non-critical emails

### 8.2 Physical Documents
- Store in locked cabinets
- Shred confidential documents (don't just trash)
- Clean desk policy
- Visitor escort (don't leave documents unattended)

### 8.3 Digital Storage
- Use company-approved cloud storage (Google Drive, SharePoint)
- Do not use personal cloud (Dropbox, personal Google Drive)
- Encrypt sensitive files
- Set appropriate access permissions

### 8.4 Devices
- Company laptops only for work
- Strong passwords (12+ characters, complex)
- Enable full-disk encryption
- Antivirus and security patches up-to-date
- Remote wipe capability enabled

### 8.5 Printing
- Minimize printing of confidential documents
- Collect printouts immediately
- Use secure print (release at printer with PIN)
- Shred when no longer needed

## 9. Third-Party Data Sharing

### 9.1 When Sharing is Allowed
- Legal requirements (court orders, government requests)
- Service providers under contract (payroll, benefits admin)
- With customer consent (for specific purposes)

### 9.2 Vendor Management
- Data Processing Agreements (DPA) signed
- Security assessments conducted
- Audit rights retained
- Data breach notification clauses

## 10. Data Security Measures

### 10.1 Technical Controls
- **Access control**: Role-based access (RBAC)
- **Encryption**: AES-256 for data at rest, TLS 1.3 for data in transit
- **Multi-factor authentication (MFA)**: For all systems with sensitive data
- **Firewalls and IDS/IPS**: Perimeter security
- **DLP (Data Loss Prevention)**: Monitor and block unauthorized data transfers
- **Regular backups**: Daily backups, tested quarterly

### 10.2 Physical Controls
- **Badge access**: Restricted areas
- **CCTV**: Common areas (not private spaces)
- **Visitor management**: Sign-in, escort, badge
- **Secure disposal**: Shredding, certified e-waste disposal

### 10.3 Administrative Controls
- Security awareness training (annual, mandatory)
- Background verification for employees
- Confidentiality agreements
- Incident response plan
- Security audits (quarterly internal, annual external)

## 11. Incident Response

### 11.1 Data Breach Definition
- Unauthorized access to data
- Accidental data exposure
- Data theft or loss
- Ransomware or cyberattack

### 11.2 Response Steps
1. **Detect and contain**: Isolate affected systems
2. **Assess**: Determine scope and impact
3. **Notify**: Security team, management, legal
4. **Investigate**: Root cause analysis
5. **Notify stakeholders**: Customers, employees, regulators (as required)
6. **Remediate**: Fix vulnerability, restore systems
7. **Document**: Incident report, lessons learned
8. **Review**: Update policies and controls

### 11.3 Reporting
- **Internal**: Report to security@amazatic.com immediately
- **External**: To regulators within 72 hours (if required by law)
- **Customers**: Within 72 hours

## 12. Compliance

### 12.1 Indian Laws
- **Information Technology Act, 2000**
- **IT (Reasonable Security Practices) Rules, 2011**
- **Personal Data Protection Bill** (when enacted)

### 12.2 International Laws
- **GDPR** (for EU customers/employees)
- **CCPA** (for California customers)
- Industry-specific (HIPAA for healthcare data, PCI-DSS for payment data)

### 12.3 Audits and Certifications
- **ISO 27001** (Information Security Management)
- **SOC 2 Type II** (Security, Availability, Confidentiality)
- Annual compliance audits
- Penetration testing (bi-annual)

## 13. Employee Monitoring

### 13.1 What is Monitored
- Work email (for security and legal purposes)
- Internet usage (company network)
- Access logs (systems accessed, times)
- Time and attendance
- CCTV in common areas

### 13.2 Purpose
- Security threat detection
- Legal compliance
- Investigation of misconduct
- Productivity monitoring (transparent)

### 13.3 Employee Rights
- Informed about monitoring
- No monitoring in private spaces (restrooms, changing rooms, private cabins)
- No personal communications monitoring without legal cause

## 14. Social Media and Public Communications

### 14.1 Personal Use
- Do not share confidential company information
- Do not speak on behalf of company (unless authorized)
- Disclaimer: "Views are my own"
- No defamation of company, colleagues, or clients

### 14.2 Professional Use
- Authorized employees only for company social media
- Social media policy training
- Pre-approval for content
- Crisis management plan

## 15. Remote Work and BYOD

### 15.1 Work From Home
- Use company VPN
- Secure home WiFi (WPA3, strong password)
- Private workspace (away from family/guests)
- Screen privacy (no shoulder-surfing)

### 15.2 Bring Your Own Device (BYOD)
- Discouraged for access to confidential data
- If allowed: MDM (Mobile Device Management) software installed
- Company can remote-wipe business data
- Separate business and personal data

## 16. Consequences of Violation

### 16.1 Disciplinary Action
- **Minor violation**: Warning, retraining
- **Serious violation**: Suspension, termination
- **Gross violation**: Immediate termination, legal action

### 16.2 Legal Consequences
- Civil lawsuit for damages
- Criminal charges (under IT Act)
- Non-compete and NDA enforcement

## 17. Training and Awareness

### 17.1 Mandatory Training
- Data privacy and security: At joining
- Refresher training: Annual
- Specialized training: For roles handling sensitive data (HR, Finance)

### 17.2 Awareness Programs
- Monthly security tips via email
- Phishing simulation exercises
- Data Privacy Day events
- Posters and reminders

## 18. Data Subject Requests

### 18.1 Request Process
- Email: privacy@amazatic.com
- Request form available on intranet
- Identity verification required
- Response within 30 days

### 18.2 Types of Requests
- Access (copy of data)
- Correction (update data)
- Deletion (erase data)
- Portability (transfer data)
- Objection (stop processing)

## 19. Privacy by Design

### 19.1 Principles
- Privacy considered from design stage
- Data minimization
- Purpose limitation
- Transparency
- User control

### 19.2 Privacy Impact Assessment (PIA)
- Conducted for new projects involving personal data
- Identify and mitigate privacy risks
- Documented and approved before launch

## 20. Roles and Responsibilities

### 20.1 Data Protection Officer (DPO)
- Oversees data protection strategy
- Ensures compliance
- Point of contact for regulators and data subjects
- Reports to CEO

### 20.2 IT Security Team
- Implement security controls
- Monitor for threats
- Respond to incidents
- Conduct audits

### 20.3 All Employees
- Follow data handling policies
- Report incidents immediately
- Complete training
- Safeguard credentials
