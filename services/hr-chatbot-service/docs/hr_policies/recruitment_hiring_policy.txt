# Recruitment and Hiring Policy

**Company**: Amazatic Technologies
**Effective Date**: January 2025
**Version**: 3.0

## 1. Purpose and Scope

This policy establishes guidelines for recruiting, selecting, and hiring qualified candidates who align with Amazatic Technologies' values, culture, and business objectives.

**Applies to**: All departments, hiring managers, HR team, and candidates

## 2. Recruitment Principles

### 2.1 Equal Opportunity Employment
- Merit-based selection
- No discrimination based on race, gender, age, religion, disability, or sexual orientation
- Compliance with local labor laws and regulations
- Fair and transparent process

### 2.2 Hiring Authority
- Department heads: Approve hiring needs
- HR: Manages recruitment process
- Hiring managers: Conduct interviews
- CEO/COO: Final approval for senior positions

## 3. Workforce Planning

### 3.1 Annual Planning
- Q4: Department heads submit hiring plans for next year
- Budget approval required
- Headcount caps defined
- Quarterly review and adjustments

### 3.2 Unplanned Hiring
- Business case required
- Budget reallocation
- Emergency hiring (resignation, termination)
- Approval from department head and CFO

## 4. Position Creation and Approval

### 4.1 Job Description
- Title and department
- Reporting relationship
- Key responsibilities (5-10 points)
- Required qualifications (education, experience, skills)
- Preferred qualifications
- Compensation range
- Location (office/remote/hybrid)

### 4.2 Approval Workflow
1. Hiring manager creates job description
2. HR reviews for compliance and market standards
3. Department head approves
4. Finance approves budget
5. HR publishes job posting

**Timeline**: 5-7 business days for approval

## 5. Sourcing Strategies

### 5.1 Internal Recruitment
- **First Priority**: Internal candidates
- Post on company intranet for 7 days
- Current employees can apply
- Referral bonus: ₹50,000 for successful hires
- Internal transfers: Minimum 1 year in current role

### 5.2 External Recruitment
- Company career portal
- Job boards (LinkedIn, Naukri, Indeed)
- Campus recruitment (IITs, NITs, top colleges)
- Professional networking
- Recruitment agencies (senior roles only)
- Social media campaigns

### 5.3 Recruitment Agencies
- **When to use**: Senior positions, specialized roles, urgent hiring
- **Fees**: 8-15% of annual CTC
- **Replacement guarantee**: 90 days
- **Approved vendors**: Pre-qualified agencies only

## 6. Candidate Experience

### 6.1 Application Process
- Online application form (10 minutes to complete)
- Resume/CV upload (PDF, DOC formats)
- Cover letter (optional)
- LinkedIn profile (preferred)
- Portfolio (for design/creative roles)

### 6.2 Communication
- Acknowledgment within 24 hours
- Status updates every 7 days
- Personalized rejection emails
- Feedback for final round candidates
- Average response time: 3-5 business days

### 6.3 Application Tracking System (ATS)
- All applications tracked in HRMS
- Hiring pipeline visibility
- Automated communications
- Compliance documentation
- Analytics and reporting

## 7. Screening and Assessment

### 7.1 Resume Screening
- **Criteria**: Education, experience, skills match
- **Timeline**: Within 3 business days
- **Outcome**: Shortlist 5-10 candidates per position
- **Communication**: Email to shortlisted candidates

### 7.2 Phone Screening (Round 1)
- **Duration**: 15-20 minutes
- **Conducted by**: HR recruiter
- **Topics**: Background, motivation, salary expectations, notice period
- **Outcome**: Shortlist for technical interview

### 7.3 Technical Assessment (Round 2)
- **Duration**: 60-90 minutes
- **Format**: Coding test, case study, presentation, or practical assignment
- **Evaluated by**: Technical team lead
- **Scoring**: Standardized rubric (0-100)
- **Pass threshold**: 70%

### 7.4 Technical Interview (Round 3)
- **Duration**: 60 minutes
- **Panel**: Hiring manager + 1-2 team members
- **Focus**: Technical skills, problem-solving, domain knowledge
- **Format**: Live coding, system design, technical discussion
- **Evaluation**: Competency-based assessment

### 7.5 Managerial Interview (Round 4)
- **Duration**: 45 minutes
- **Conducted by**: Department head
- **Focus**: Cultural fit, leadership potential, team collaboration
- **Format**: Behavioral questions (STAR method)
- **Topics**: Past experiences, conflict resolution, achievements

### 7.6 HR Interview (Round 5)
- **Duration**: 30 minutes
- **Conducted by**: HR manager
- **Topics**: Company culture, benefits, salary negotiation, joining formalities
- **Documents**: Verify education, experience certificates
- **Offer discussion**: Compensation, benefits, joining date

## 8. Interview Guidelines

### 8.1 Interview Preparation
- Review candidate's resume and assessment
- Prepare structured questions
- Define evaluation criteria
- Ensure uninterrupted time and space
- Use video conferencing for remote candidates

### 8.2 Prohibited Questions
- Age, marital status, family planning
- Religious or political beliefs
- Health conditions (except job-related)
- Financial status
- Sexual orientation

### 8.3 Interview Evaluation
- Complete within 24 hours of interview
- Standardized scoring (1-5 scale)
- Written feedback required
- Collaborative decision-making
- Consensus for hiring decision

## 9. Background Verification

### 9.1 Mandatory Checks
- **Education verification**: Degrees, certifications
- **Employment verification**: Last 2 employers, roles, tenure
- **Reference checks**: 2 professional references
- **Identity verification**: Government ID, address proof
- **Criminal background check**: For all positions

### 9.2 Additional Checks (Senior Roles)
- Credit check (finance roles)
- Social media screening
- Drug testing (specific roles)
- Driving record (if driving required)

### 9.3 Process
- **Timing**: After offer acceptance, before joining
- **Vendor**: Third-party verification agency
- **Timeline**: 7-10 business days
- **Action on discrepancy**: Offer withdrawn if major discrepancies found

## 10. Offer Management

### 10.1 Offer Components
- Job title and department
- Reporting manager
- Annual CTC (Cost to Company) breakdown
- Fixed salary (monthly)
- Variable pay (performance bonus)
- Stock options (if applicable)
- Benefits (health insurance, PF, gratuity)
- Joining date
- Probation period: 6 months
- Notice period: 60 days (post-probation)
- Work location
- Offer validity: 7 days

### 10.2 Offer Approval
1. HR prepares offer letter
2. Department head approves
3. Finance approves (budget check)
4. CHRO/CEO approves (for senior roles)
5. HR sends offer to candidate

**Timeline**: 2-3 business days

### 10.3 Salary Negotiation
- Negotiation window: ±10% of initial offer
- Based on experience and market data
- Final approval from department head
- No negotiation on benefits (standardized)

### 10.4 Offer Acceptance
- Candidate signs offer letter
- Returns scanned copy via email
- Confirmation within 7 days
- Joining date agreed (typically 30-60 days)

## 11. Campus Recruitment

### 11.1 Target Institutions
- **Tier 1**: IITs, IISC, NITs (top 10)
- **Tier 2**: Top private engineering colleges
- **Tier 3**: Top B-schools for MBA roles

### 11.2 Campus Process
- Pre-placement talk (45 minutes)
- Written test (aptitude + technical)
- Group discussion (for MBA candidates)
- Technical interview
- HR interview
- Offer on campus or within 48 hours

### 11.3 Fresher Package
- **Entry-level Engineer**: ₹6-10 LPA
- **Senior Engineer (PG)**: ₹12-18 LPA
- **MBA (Business roles)**: ₹10-15 LPA
- **Probation**: 6 months
- **Bond**: 2 years (with repayment clause if leaving early)

## 12. Internship Program

### 12.1 Types of Internships
- **Summer Internship**: 2-3 months (May-July)
- **Winter Internship**: 2-3 months (Dec-Jan)
- **Industrial Training**: 6 months (BTech final year)

### 12.2 Eligibility
- Students from target institutions
- Minimum GPA: 7.0/10
- Relevant skills/coursework

### 12.3 Stipend
- ₹15,000 - ₹25,000 per month
- Based on skills and college tier

### 12.4 Pre-Placement Offer (PPO)
- Top performing interns (top 30%)
- Full-time offer upon graduation
- Same package as campus hire
- Validity: 1 year

## 13. Onboarding Process

### 13.1 Pre-Joining
- Welcome email with joining details
- Document submission list
- First-day agenda
- IT setup coordination
- Buddy assignment

### 13.2 First Day
- Welcome by HR
- Office tour
- ID card and access cards
- IT equipment (laptop, phone if applicable)
- Introduction to team
- HR orientation (2 hours)

### 13.3 First Week
- Department orientation
- Training schedule
- Meet key stakeholders
- Goal setting with manager
- HRMS portal training

### 13.4 First Month
- Role-specific training
- Regular check-ins with manager
- Buddy meetings
- HR check-in (feedback session)

### 13.5 Probation Review
- **3-month review**: Mid-probation feedback
- **6-month review**: Final probation evaluation
- **Confirmation**: Probation cleared or extended/terminated

## 14. Diversity and Inclusion

### 14.1 Commitment
- Diverse hiring panels
- Blind resume screening (remove personal info)
- Inclusive job descriptions (avoid gender-biased language)
- Partnerships with diversity organizations

### 14.2 Goals
- Gender diversity: Target 40% women in workforce
- Leadership diversity: Diverse representation at all levels
- Disability hiring: 3% of workforce

## 15. Recruitment Metrics

### 15.1 Key Performance Indicators (KPIs)
- Time to hire: Average 30 days
- Cost per hire: ₹50,000
- Offer acceptance rate: >80%
- New hire retention (1 year): >85%
- Quality of hire (performance rating): >3.5/5
- Diversity hiring rate: 40%

### 15.2 Reporting
- Weekly recruitment pipeline updates
- Monthly hiring dashboard
- Quarterly diversity report
- Annual recruitment report

## 16. Compliance and Documentation

### 16.1 Legal Compliance
- Labor laws (Contract Labour Act, Shops and Establishments Act)
- Equal opportunity laws
- Data privacy (candidate data protection)
- Immigration laws (for international hires)

### 16.2 Document Retention
- Applications: 1 year
- Interview evaluations: 2 years
- Offer letters: Permanent
- Background verification: Permanent

## 17. Recruitment Budget

### 17.1 Budget Components
- Job board subscriptions
- Recruitment agency fees
- Campus recruitment costs
- Referral bonuses
- Recruitment marketing
- ATS/HRMS license

### 17.2 Budget Approval
- Annual budget in Q4
- Quarterly review and reallocation
- Emergency budget requests

## 18. Exceptions and Escalations

### 18.1 Fast-Track Hiring
- Critical business need
- Accelerated process (15-day target)
- Senior leadership approval
- Premium compensation allowed

### 18.2 Rehiring (Boomerang Employees)
- Left in good standing (voluntary resignation)
- Minimum 1 year gap
- Manager and HR approval
- Simplified interview process
- Joining bonus waived
