# Health and Safety Policy

**Company**: Amazatic Technologies
**Effective Date**: January 2025
**Version**: 1.5

## 1. Purpose

Amazatic Technologies is committed to providing a safe and healthy work environment for all employees, contractors, and visitors. This policy outlines safety standards, procedures, and responsibilities.

## 2. Health and Safety Commitment

### 2.1 Management Commitment
- Zero tolerance for safety violations
- Continuous improvement of safety standards
- Regular risk assessments
- Safety training for all employees
- Compliance with Occupational Safety and Health regulations

### 2.2 Employee Responsibility
- Follow all safety procedures
- Report hazards immediately
- Use safety equipment provided
- Participate in safety training
- Look out for colleagues' safety

## 3. Workplace Safety

### 3.1 Physical Safety
- **Ergonomic workstations**: Adjustable chairs, proper desk height, monitor placement
- **Lighting**: Adequate natural and artificial lighting
- **Temperature control**: AC maintained at 22-24°C
- **Air quality**: Proper ventilation and air purification systems
- **Noise levels**: Quiet zones, noise-cancelling options

### 3.2 Fire Safety
- **Fire extinguishers**: On every floor, inspected monthly
- **Fire alarms**: Tested quarterly
- **Emergency exits**: Clearly marked, never blocked
- **Fire drills**: Conducted twice a year
- **Assembly points**: Designated safe areas outside building
- **Fire wardens**: Trained employees on each floor

### 3.3 Electrical Safety
- Regular inspection of electrical equipment
- No overloading of power outlets
- Report frayed wires or damaged equipment immediately
- Only certified electricians for repairs
- Unplug devices when not in use

### 3.4 Housekeeping
- Clutter-free walkways and emergency exits
- Spills cleaned immediately
- Cables and wires organized (no tripping hazards)
- Regular cleaning and pest control
- Waste disposal in designated bins

## 4. Health and Wellness

### 4.1 Medical Facilities
- **On-site medical room**: First aid, basic medications
- **Nurse on duty**: 9 AM to 6 PM on weekdays
- **Tie-ups with hospitals**: For emergencies
- **Ambulance service**: On call 24×7

### 4.2 First Aid
- First aid kits on every floor
- Trained first aiders (2 per floor)
- Contact list displayed prominently
- Monthly replenishment of supplies

### 4.3 Mental Health
- Employee Assistance Program (EAP): Free counseling (5 sessions per year)
- Mental health awareness sessions
- Stress management workshops
- Flexible work arrangements for mental health needs
- Confidential support

### 4.4 Ergonomics
- Ergonomic assessments available
- Adjustable chairs and desks
- Monitor stands and laptop risers
- External keyboard and mouse for laptop users
- Eye care: Anti-glare screens, 20-20-20 rule (every 20 mins, look 20 feet away for 20 seconds)

## 5. Emergency Procedures

### 5.1 Fire Emergency
1. **Alert**: Activate fire alarm
2. **Evacuate**: Leave via nearest exit (don't use elevators)
3. **Assemble**: At designated assembly point
4. **Roll call**: Fire wardens conduct headcount
5. **Wait**: For all-clear from fire department

**Do not**:
- Use elevators
- Collect belongings
- Re-enter building

### 5.2 Medical Emergency
1. Call on-site nurse (Ext. 111)
2. Call ambulance if serious (108 or tied-up hospital)
3. Provide first aid if trained
4. Notify HR and employee's emergency contact
5. Accompany employee to hospital if needed

### 5.3 Earthquake
- **During**: Drop, Cover, and Hold (under desk)
- **After**: Evacuate cautiously, watch for aftershocks
- Assemble at open area away from buildings

### 5.4 Active Shooter / Security Threat
- **Run**: Evacuate if safe to do so
- **Hide**: Lock doors, turn off lights, silence phones
- **Fight**: Last resort, use any means to defend
- Call security (Ext. 100) and police (100)

## 6. COVID-19 and Infectious Disease Protocol

### 6.1 Prevention
- Hand sanitizers at every entry point and on desks
- Masks available (voluntary unless mandate)
- Social distancing norms during outbreaks
- Regular sanitization of common areas
- Touchless attendance systems

### 6.2 Symptoms Reporting
- Report symptoms immediately (fever, cough, cold)
- Work from home if unwell
- Testing facility available on-site
- Sick leave without medical certificate (for flu-like symptoms)

### 6.3 Positive Case Protocol
- Isolate immediately
- Contact tracing conducted
- Deep sanitization of area
- Notify employees who were in contact (anonymously)
- Work from home for close contacts

## 7. Substance Abuse Policy

### 7.1 Alcohol and Drugs
- **Prohibited**: Being under the influence at work
- **Zero tolerance**: For illegal drugs
- **Prescription medications**: Notify manager if it affects job performance

### 7.2 Smoking
- Smoking prohibited inside office premises
- Designated smoking zones outside building
- Vaping treated same as smoking
- Smoking cessation support available

### 7.3 Consequences
- First offense: Warning
- Second offense: Suspension
- Third offense: Termination

## 8. Occupational Health

### 8.1 Annual Health Checkups
- Free comprehensive health checkup for all employees
- At empanelled hospitals
- Includes blood tests, ECG, chest X-ray, doctor consultation
- Reports confidential (only employee receives)

### 8.2 Vision Care
- Eye checkups every 6 months for employees with computer-intensive roles
- Subsidy for prescription glasses (₹5,000 per year)
- Blue light filter lenses recommended

### 8.3 Workplace Injuries
- Report all injuries, however minor
- Document in incident register
- Medical treatment provided
- Workers' compensation as per law
- Investigation to prevent recurrence

### 8.4 Repetitive Strain Injury (RSI) Prevention
- Ergonomic training
- Regular breaks (5 min every hour)
- Stretching exercises
- Physiotherapy support if needed

## 9. Travel Safety

### 9.1 Domestic Travel
- Fly with reputed airlines
- Stay in safe hotels (company-approved)
- Avoid late-night travel alone
- Share itinerary with manager
- Travel insurance provided

### 9.2 International Travel
- Check travel advisories
- Vaccinations if required
- International health insurance
- Emergency contact: 24×7 hotline
- Security briefing for high-risk destinations

### 9.3 Commute Safety
- Cab service for late working hours (post 8 PM)
- Background-verified drivers
- GPS tracking of cabs
- Emergency button in cab app
- Women employees: Preference for evening cab service

## 10. Security

### 10.1 Physical Security
- Security guards at all entry/exit points
- Visitor management system (check-in required)
- CCTV surveillance (common areas, not inside rooms)
- Access control (ID card required)
- Security audit annually

### 10.2 Personal Safety
- Don't share personal information with unauthorized persons
- Report suspicious activity
- Escort policy for visitors
- After-hours access: Register with security

### 10.3 Workplace Violence
- Zero tolerance policy
- Includes physical violence, threats, intimidation
- Report to security and HR immediately
- Investigation and action within 24 hours
- Legal action if warranted

- Near misses
- Safety hazards
- Security concerns

### 11.2 Reporting Process
1. Immediate: Notify supervisor or security
2. Document: Fill incident report form (on HRMS)
3. Investigation: Safety officer investigates
4. Action: Corrective measures implemented
5. Follow-up: Ensure issue resolved

### 18.3 Continuous Work
