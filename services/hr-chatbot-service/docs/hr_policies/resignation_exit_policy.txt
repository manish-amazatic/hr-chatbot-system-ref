# Resignation and Exit Policy

**Company**: Amazatic Technologies
**Effective Date**: January 2025
**Version**: 2.0

## 1. Purpose

This policy outlines the procedures for voluntary resignations, exits, and offboarding to ensure smooth transitions and maintain positive relationships with departing employees.

## 2. Notice Period

### 2.1 Standard Notice Period
- **Probation period (0-6 months)**: 30 days
- **Confirmed employees (post-probation)**: 60 days
- **Manager and above**: 90 days
- **Senior leadership**: 90 days or as per contract

### 2.2 Notice Period Buyout
- **Employee request**: Pay in lieu of notice (1 month salary per month)
- **Company approval**: Required from department head and HR
- **Processing**: Full and final settlement within 15 days

### 2.3 Waiver of Notice
- **Company discretion**: May release employee earlier
- **Reasons**: Redundancy, mutual agreement, performance issues
- **No payment**: If company initiates early release

## 3. Resignation Process

### 3.1 Resignation Submission
**Step 1**: Email resignation to manager and HR (resignation@amazatic.com)
**Step 2**: Resignation letter must include:
- Last working date (after notice period)
- Brief reason for leaving (optional)
- Signature

**Step 3**: HR acknowledges receipt within 24 hours

### 3.2 Resignation Acceptance
- Manager and HR review
- Acceptance letter issued within 3 business days
- Confirms last working date
- Outlines exit formalities

### 3.3 Counter Offer
- Manager may make counter offer if employee is valuable
- **Components**: Salary increase, promotion, role change, project assignment
- **Employee decision**: Within 3 business days
- **Acceptance**: Withdraw resignation in writing
- **Decline**: Exit process continues

## 4. Exit Formalities

### 4.1 Knowledge Transfer (KT)
- **Duration**: Entire notice period
- **Components**:
  - Document ongoing projects and status
  - Prepare handover notes
  - Train replacement or team member
  - Share access to files/systems
- **Handover meeting**: With manager and team
- **Completion certificate**: Manager signs off

### 4.2 Return of Company Property
Items to return before last day:
- Laptop, mouse, keyboard, accessories
- Mobile phone (if company-provided)
- ID card, access cards, building passes
- Documents, files, hard drives
- Keys, parking pass
- Company credit card
- Books from company library
- Any other company property

**Process**: IT and Admin verify and sign off

### 4.3 Access Revocation
- **Last working day**: All system access revoked
- Email, HRMS, VPN, project tools
- Building access disabled
- Remote access disabled

### 4.4 Exit Interview
- **Conducted by**: HR manager
- **Duration**: 30-45 minutes
- **Purpose**: Understand reasons for leaving, gather feedback
- **Topics**:
  - Reason for resignation
  - Work experience at company
  - Manager relationship
  - Career growth opportunities
  - Salary and benefits satisfaction
  - Suggestions for improvement
- **Confidential**: Feedback aggregated, not attributed

## 5. Final Settlement

### 5.1 Components
- **Salary**: Prorated for days worked in final month
- **Leave encashment**: Unused annual leave balance (max 5 days)
- **Variable pay**: Prorated annual bonus (if applicable)
- **Notice period recovery**: Deducted if short notice
- **Loan recovery**: Any outstanding advances/loans deducted

### 5.2 Deductions
- Notice period shortfall (if applicable)
- Any outstanding dues (laptop damage, etc.)
- Advances and loans

### 5.3 Payment Timeline
- **With full notice period**: Within 30 days of last working day
- **Buyout/short notice**: Within 45 days

### 5.4 Documents Provided
- Full and final settlement statement
- Experience letter
- Relieving letter
- Form 16 (for tax)
- Service certificate
- PF withdrawal forms (if required)

## 6. Experience and Relieving Letters

### 6.1 Experience Letter
- **Content**: Designation, tenure, brief responsibilities
- **Issuance**: Within 7 days of last working day
- **Format**: Company letterhead, signed by HR

### 6.2 Relieving Letter
- **Content**: Confirmation of relieving, last working date, clearance
- **Issuance**: On last working day (after all clearances)
- **Important**: Required for joining new company

## 7. References and Background Verification

### 7.1 Reference Checks
- Future employers may contact HR for reference
- HR provides: Designation, tenure, employment dates
- **Not provided**: Salary details, performance ratings (without employee consent)

### 7.2 Background Verification
- Company may participate in BGV for ex-employees
- Verification: Employment dates, designation, reason for exit
- No negative comments unless serious misconduct

## 8. Post-Exit Obligations

### 8.1 Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)
- Remains valid after exit
- Cannot share confidential information
- Trade secrets, client data, proprietary code
- **Penalty**: Legal action, damages

### 8.2 Non-Compete Clause
- **Duration**: 6-12 months (as per employment contract)
- **Restrictions**: Cannot join direct competitors, solicit clients/employees
- **Exceptions**: Mutual agreement to waive

### 8.3 Intellectual Property
- All work created during employment belongs to company
- Cannot use company code, designs, ideas in new role
- Cannot claim ownership post-exit

## 9. Rehire Policy

### 9.1 Eligibility
- Left in good standing (voluntary resignation)
- No disciplinary issues
- Minimum 12 months gap
- Open position available

### 9.2 Rehire Process
- Apply through careers portal or contact HR
- Simplified interview process (1-2 rounds)
- Reference from previous manager
- HR and department head approval

### 9.3 Rehire Benefits
- Previous service not counted for leave accrual or long-service benefits
- Fresh probation period: 3 months (instead of 6)
- Market-rate salary (previous salary not considered)

## 10. Retirement

### 10.1 Retirement Age
- **Standard retirement age**: 60 years
- **Extension**: Case-by-case for critical roles (max 2 years)

### 10.2 Retirement Process
- Notify HR 6 months in advance
- Phased knowledge transfer
- Retirement farewell event
- Retirement gifts and recognition

### 10.3 Retirement Benefits
- **Gratuity**: As per Payment of Gratuity Act
- **Provident Fund**: Full PF balance
- **Leave encashment**: All unused leave
- **Pension**: If enrolled in superannuation

## 11. Exit Types

### 11.1 Voluntary Resignation
- Employee-initiated
- Standard notice period applies
- Full benefits upon exit

### 11.2 Retirement
- After completing service tenure or reaching age
- Enhanced benefits
- Special recognition

### 11.3 End of Contract
- Fixed-term contracts ending
- Mutual decision not to renew
- 30 days notice or as per contract

### 11.4 Absconding
- Unauthorized absence for 3+ consecutive days
- No intimation to manager
- **Action**:
  - Termination for abandonment of employment
  - No relieving letter
  - Notice period dues recovered
  - Negative reference

## 12. Clearance Certificate

### 12.1 Department Clearances Required
- **IT**: Return all equipment and accessories
- **Admin**: Return ID card, access cards, keys
- **Finance**: Clear any outstanding dues or advances
- **Library**: Return books
- **Manager**: Knowledge transfer sign-off

### 12.2 Final Clearance
- All departments must sign clearance form
- HR issues No Objection Certificate (NOC)
- Final settlement processed only after clearances

## 13. Alumni Network

### 13.1 Amazatic Alumni Program
- All ex-employees automatically enrolled
- LinkedIn group for networking
- Invitations to company events
- Quarterly newsletter

### 13.2 Benefits
- Referral bonuses continue (if you refer candidates)
- Rehire preference
- Networking opportunities
- Access to company events (select occasions)

## 14. Manager Checklist

When an employee resigns:
- [ ] Acknowledge resignation immediately
- [ ] Forward to HR
- [ ] Discuss reasons and possibility of retention
- [ ] Plan knowledge transfer
- [ ] Identify replacement or redistribute work
- [ ] Conduct exit interview or provide input to HR
- [ ] Sign clearance after KT completion
- [ ] Wish employee well

## 15. Employee Checklist

Before last working day:
- [ ] Submit resignation email
- [ ] Serve notice period (or buyout)
- [ ] Complete knowledge transfer
- [ ] Prepare handover document
- [ ] Return all company property
- [ ] Complete exit interview
- [ ] Obtain clearances from all departments
- [ ] Collect experience and relieving letters
- [ ] Verify final settlement amount
- [ ] Update personal email for future communication
